Parisian-Style Steak Frites

Description

Bring the romance of a Parisian brasserie to your table with this steak frites. Juicy hanger steaks are draped in a silky sauce made with white wine, beef broth, and umami-rich anchovies, finished with a generous pat of fresh herb butter that melts over each bite.

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons vegetable oil, or as needed
  • 4 hanger steaks
  • 4 tablespoons minced shallot
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ½ cup beef broth
  • 4 anchovy fillets, chopped, or more to taste
  • 6 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons minced fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons minced fresh chervil
  • 2 teaspoons minced fresh thyme

Steps

  1. Sear the Steaks: Pat the hanger steaks dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and pepper. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add the steaks and sear for 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare, or adjust to your preferred doneness. Transfer the steaks to a plate, tent loosely with foil, and let rest while you make the sauce.

  2. Build the Pan Sauce: In the same skillet, reduce heat to medium and add the minced shallots. Cook for 1-2 minutes until softened, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes until reduced by half. Add the beef broth and chopped anchovies, stirring until the anchovies dissolve into the sauce.

  3. Finish with Herb Butter: Remove the pan from heat and stir in the butter, one tablespoon at a time, swirling the pan until it melts into a silky sauce. Add the minced parsley, chervil, and thyme, stirring to combine. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.

  4. Plate and Serve: Place each steak on a warm plate and spoon the herb butter sauce generously over the top. Serve immediately with crispy French fries (frites) and a simple green salad for an authentic Parisian bistro experience.
