Sesame-Seared Tuna

Description

This sesame-crusted tuna comes together fast and tastes incredible. Just coat fresh tuna steaks in sesame seeds, sear them quickly, and enjoy them rare.
Make sure to grab high-quality fresh tuna from your fishmonger.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • 4 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons mirin (Japanese sweet wine)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • tablespoons rice wine vinegar
  • 1 cup sesame seeds
  • (6 ounce) tuna steaks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • wasabi paste

Steps

  1. Make the Marinade: Whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, mirin, honey, and rice wine vinegar in a medium bowl until the honey is fully dissolved and the mixture is well combined.

  2. Marinate the Tuna: Place the tuna steaks in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, turning to coat all sides. Let them marinate for 15-20 minutes at room temperature, flipping once halfway through.

  3. Prepare the Sesame Coating: Spread the sesame seeds in a shallow plate or dish. Remove the tuna steaks from the marinade, letting any excess drip off (reserve the marinade if desired for serving).

  4. Coat the Tuna: Press each tuna steak firmly into the sesame seeds on both sides, ensuring an even, generous coating that adheres well to the fish.

  5. Heat the Pan: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or cast-iron pan over high heat until it's very hot and almost smoking. This ensures a proper sear.

  6. Sear the Tuna: Carefully place the sesame-crusted tuna steaks in the hot pan. Sear for 1-2 minutes per side for rare, or slightly longer if you prefer medium-rare. The tuna should be golden brown on the outside and still pink in the center.

  7. Slice and Serve: Remove the tuna from heat and let it rest for 1 minute. Slice each steak against the grain into ½-inch thick pieces. Serve immediately with wasabi paste on the side.
